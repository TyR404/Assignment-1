Overview

PlayStation (officially abbreviated as PS) is a video game brand that is produced by Sony Interactive Entertainment, a division of Sony. PlayStation has released a large variety of products. These products consist of five home video game consoles, two handheld video game consoles, a media center, a smartphone, as well as an online service and multiple magazines.



Since the initial release of the original PlayStation in 1994, there have been 111 million registered users and approximately 576.1 million consoles sold.



Backstory


Until 1991, Sony had little direct involvement with the video game industry. The company supplied components for other consoles, such as the sound chip for the Super Famicom from Nintendo, and operated a video game studio, Sony Imagesoft.



After working together for 3 years, Nintendo broke off its joint deal with PlayStation, opting to go with Philips instead. The breaking of the partnership infuriated Sony President Norio Ohga, who responded by appointing Ken Kutaragi (The Father of the PlayStation) with the responsibility of developing the PlayStation project to rival Nintendo
